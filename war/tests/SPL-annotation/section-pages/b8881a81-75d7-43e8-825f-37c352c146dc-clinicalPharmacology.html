CLINICAL PHARMACOLOGY

Protriptyline hydrochloride is an antidepressant agent. The
mechanism of its antidepressant action in man is not known. It is not a
monoamine oxidase inhibitor, and it does not act primarily by stimulation of the
central nervous system.
Protriptyline has been found in some studies to have a more rapid onset of
action than imipramine or amitriptyline. The initial clinical effect may occur
within one week. Sedative and tranquilizing properties are lacking. The rate of
excretion is slow.
